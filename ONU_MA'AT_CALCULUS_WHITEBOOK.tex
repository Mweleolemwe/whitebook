\begin{abstract}
The global regularity of solutions to the 3D Navier--Stokes equations (NSE) remains a central challenge in mathematical physics. This work introduces the Onu--Ma'at Synthesis, a scale-covariant analytical framework that reinterprets singularity formation as a consequence of coordinate underdetermination. By mapping the fluid domain to a logarithmic ``Rail'' gauge ($s = \ln r$) and introducing a unique class of Haar-invariant regulators—the Onu Breathing Operators—we demonstrate that pathological energy concentrations are reclassified as bounded scale-flux transport events. This synthesis provides a stable analytical, numerical, and hardware-native platform for multi-scale fluid modeling.
\end{abstract}
