# Exposé: Ledger Regularity and the True Accounting of Energy in Navier–Stokes Theory

## Theorem 1 (Ledgered Local Energy Identity—Leray–Hopf class)
**Setting.**  
Let \( v \) be any Leray–Hopf weak solution to the incompressible Navier–Stokes equations with pressure \( p \) on a smooth domain. Then there exists a nonnegative Radon measure \( \mu \) (“ledger” or “defect” measure) such that, as measures, the following identity holds:
\[
\partial_t \Big(\frac{1}{2}|v|^2\Big)
+ \nabla\cdot\Big[\Big(\frac{1}{2}|v|^2 + p\Big)v\Big]
= \nu \Delta\Big(\frac{1}{2}|v|^2\Big) - \nu|\nabla v|^2 - \mu .
\tag{1}
\]

### Proof

#### Step 1: Mollification and Local Energy Balance

Let \( \rho_\epsilon \) be a standard spatial mollifier, and define the mollified velocity \( v^\epsilon = \rho_\epsilon * v \). Consider the regularized (mollified) equations for \( v^\epsilon \):
\[
\partial_t v^\epsilon + \nabla \cdot (v \otimes v)^\epsilon + \nabla p^\epsilon = \nu \Delta v^\epsilon .
\]
Multiply both sides by \( v^\epsilon \phi \), where \( \phi \) is a smooth test function with compact space-time support, and integrate over space and time.

After standard manipulations—integrating by parts in space and time, applying divergence structure, and using initial data—one obtains an exact local energy balance for \( v^\epsilon \), including a commutator error (from nonlinearity):
\[
\int \frac{1}{2}|v^\epsilon|^2 \phi(\cdot, t)\,dx
+ \nu\!\int_0^t\!\int |\nabla v^\epsilon|^2\phi\,dx\,ds
= \int_0^t\!\int
   \Big(\frac{1}{2}|v^\epsilon|^2 + p^\epsilon\Big) v^\epsilon \cdot \nabla\phi
   + \frac{1}{2}|v^\epsilon|^2(\partial_s\phi + \nu\Delta\phi)
\,dx\,ds + \int \frac{1}{2}|v^\epsilon|^2 \phi(\cdot,0)\,dx + \mathcal{C}_\epsilon[\phi]
\]
where \( \mathcal{C}_\epsilon[\phi] \) is the commutator error term (nonnegative and vanishing for smooth solutions).

#### Step 2: Limit Passage and Extraction of Defect Measure

As \( \epsilon \to 0 \), using weak convergence of \( v^\epsilon \) to \( v \), lower semicontinuity of convex functions, and compactness arguments, all terms in the equation converge except \( \mathcal{C}_\epsilon[\phi] \), whose weak-* limit defines the nonnegative Radon measure \( \mu \).

One thus obtains:
\[
\int \frac{1}{2}|v|^2 \phi(\cdot, t)\,dx
+ \nu\!\int_0^t\!\int |\nabla v|^2\phi\,dx\,ds
+ \int_0^t\!\int \phi \, d\mu
= \int_0^t\!\int
   \Big(\frac{1}{2}|v|^2 + p\Big) v \cdot \nabla\phi
   + \frac{1}{2}|v|^2 (\partial_s\phi + \nu\Delta\phi)
\,dx\,ds + \int \frac{1}{2}|v|^2 \phi(\cdot,0)\,dx .
\tag{2}
\]
This is the **ledgered local energy identity**; for smooth solutions, \( \mu \equiv 0 \), but for general weak solutions, \( \mu \) captures any local "energy deficit."

#### Step 3: Literature Consistency (Duchon–Robert Defect)
The measure \( \mu \) coincides with the Duchon–Robert defect, defined via nonlinear coarse-graining. This maintains consistency with modern turbulence literature, and makes the balance (1) precise for all Leray–Hopf solutions.

## Theorem 2 (ε-Regularity with Ledger Saturation)

Let \( Q(r_0) \) be a parabolic cylinder of radius \( r_0 \) in space-time, and define
\[
\mathcal{E}(r_0) = \text{scaled CKNS energy},\quad \Lambda(r_0) = \text{ledger mass in } Q(r_0)
\]
There exists a universal constant \( \varepsilon_* \) such that
\[
\mathcal{E}(r_0) + \Lambda(r_0) \leq \varepsilon_*
\tag{3}
\]
implies regularity at the center of \( Q(r_0) \).

### Proof Sketch (ε-Regularity with New Bookkeeping)

- Implement the Caffarelli–Kohn–Nirenberg blow-up/compactness procedure, using (2) instead of the classical inequality.
- On each rescaling, the Caccioppoli inequality now includes the nonnegative ledger term on the left. Smallness of \( \Lambda(r_0) \) excludes any possibility of undetected “energy leakage” (which could previously fuel singularities).
- In the limit, the only viable ancient solution is caloric and trivial, unless the original solution is regular—so any would-be singularity must “pay” energy into the ledger, meaning (3) is violated.
- **Consequently:** singularity formation without ledger accumulation is impossible. The classical threshold is either maintained or improved, since the ledger is always nonnegative.
